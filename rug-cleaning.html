Rug Cleaning That Respects Your Investment

Rugs — particularly Persian, Oriental, and hand-knotted wool varieties — require a very different approach to wall-to-wall carpet. Their dyes can bleed, their fringes can tangle, and their backing materials can warp if treated incorrectly. Getting this wrong can cause irreversible damage to something that may be irreplaceable.

With 20 years of experience handling rugs of all types and values, we know precisely what each piece needs. We test dyes, assess fibre type, and select the gentlest effective cleaning method for your specific rug. Our equipment is calibrated for low moisture on delicate pieces to prevent distortion or dye migration.

We offer both on-site cleaning (for large, heavy rugs difficult to transport) and off-site cleaning (for smaller or particularly delicate pieces where a controlled environment is preferable).

For large, heavy rugs that are impractical to transport, on-site cleaning is the most convenient option and delivers excellent results. For smaller, very delicate, or heavily soiled rugs, an off-site clean in a controlled environment can be more thorough. We'll advise which is best for your specific rug when you call.

On-site cleaning of a standard rug typically takes 30–60 minutes. Drying takes 2–4 hours depending on pile thickness and ventilation. Off-site cleaning (including transport, wash, and return) usually takes 2–3 business days.

We clean and straighten fringes as part of our standard rug cleaning process. For damaged or knotted fringes, we can attempt to restore them during cleaning. Severely damaged fringes may need specialist rug repair — we can refer you to a trusted repairer if needed.

Shrinkage in wool rugs is caused by over-wetting combined with heat or agitation. Our low-moisture technique and controlled drying process significantly minimises this risk. We always test a hidden corner first and will advise you honestly if we feel the risk is higher than normal for a particular piece.
