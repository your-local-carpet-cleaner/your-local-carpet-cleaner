Specialist Care For Leather Furniture

Leather is durable, but it's also a living material — body oils, dust, and everyday wear gradually dull its surface and dry out the hide if it isn't cared for properly. The wrong product or a harsh scrub can crack, fade, or strip the finish entirely.

We start by identifying your leather type — aniline, semi-aniline, pigmented or suede — because each requires a different approach. From there we use pH-balanced, leather-safe cleaning agents to lift grime from the surface and out of seams and creases, followed by a conditioning treatment that restores suppleness and helps prevent future cracking.

With over 20 years caring for furniture across Sydney, Sefton, Prestons, Revesby and surrounding suburbs, we treat every piece — from a single recliner to a full lounge suite — with the same careful, considered approach.

Frequently Asked Questions

Most households get good results from a professional clean and condition every 6–12 months, depending on how much the piece is used. Furniture in high-traffic living areas, or homes with kids and pets, generally benefits from more frequent attention.

Different leathers — aniline, semi-aniline, pigmented, suede and nubuck — each need a different approach. We identify your leather type first and tailor the products and method accordingly, so delicate finishes aren't treated the same way as more robust, protected leathers.

We can treat many common marks, including ink, grease and general everyday soiling, with targeted spot treatments suited to your leather's finish. Some marks — particularly old, set-in stains or dye transfer — may only partially lift, and we'll always give you an honest assessment before we start.

Conditioning is designed to restore your leather's natural look and feel — not change it. You can expect it to look refreshed and feel softer and more supple, particularly if it had started to feel dry or stiff beforehand.

Leather generally feels dry to the touch within an hour or two, though we recommend avoiding direct sunlight or heater vents while it settles, as rapid heat-drying can cause cracking over time.
